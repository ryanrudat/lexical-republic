# Shift 4 Redesign — The `[ ].edited` App & The Insider Spy

**Status:** Design draft — 2026-05-26. Planning only, no code yet. This is the source of truth; we refine it here before building.
**Supersedes:** the current `[ ].edited` app (a word list + two dead tabs). It replaces the "open the app and find nothing to do" problem with a real spy loop woven through the shift.

---

## The idea in one sentence

> **The student is a loyal Party clerk on the outside, and a secret agent for the resistance (the Unedited) on the inside — and the surveillance work the Party forces them to do is exactly the intelligence the resistance wants.**

Same work. Two bosses. The `[ ].edited` app is the student's private channel to the resistance. Their handler is **Frey** (signs off "— F").

---

## Why we're changing it

Right now the `[ ].edited` app opens to a dictionary of five "forbidden" words and two tabs that just say *"the cipher happens during your shift"* and *"the drop box opens later."* Two of the three tabs are dead ends. A student taps around, finds nothing to actually do, and reasonably concludes it's broken. The word list also feels like a vocabulary worksheet bolted onto a resistance app — it doesn't fit the fiction.

The fix isn't to patch the tabs. It's to give the app a **job**: it's the tool you use to spy.

---

## The feeling we're going for

- **The fantasy:** you're a double agent. You do your boring government paperwork, but underneath it you're quietly noticing what the Party is hiding and smuggling it out to people who care.
- **The dread:** PEARL never stops watching. It doesn't get angry — it gets *sweet*. Think the Harry Potter character Dolores Umbridge: smiling, polite, and terrifying precisely because it never raises its voice. The horror isn't punishment; it's the cheerful concern.
- **The choice:** you decide how deep you go. Play it safe and just do your job, or take risks to learn more. Nobody is forced to be a hero.

---

## The spy loop (how a shift feels, step by step)

1. **DO YOUR JOB.** You complete normal Party tasks — matching words, reviewing documents, filing a report. This is your cover, and it's where most of the English learning lives.
2. **NOTICE.** While doing that work, you spot something the Party is quietly erasing.
3. **SNOOP (optional).** Some files in front of you are marked off-limits. You can choose to open them anyway — or leave them alone and stay clean. This is the gamble, and it's entirely your call.
4. **GET CAUGHT — maybe.** PEARL might notice you poking where you shouldn't. If it does, it interrupts you with a sweet little "interrogation," and you have to talk your way out. If it doesn't, you slip by and feel like you got away with something.
5. **FUNNEL.** Whatever you found — the thing the Party deleted, the file they hid — you send to Frey through the `[ ].edited` app.

Steps 1–2 happen to everyone. Steps 3–5 are where the student sets their own level of involvement.

---

## Who's in Shift 4

- **You / the student:** a Reconciliation Associate in the Department of Clarity. Today's job: tidy up one citizen's daily activity record for the official files.
- **Citizen-4488:** the person whose day you're "reconciling." You're surveilling them. (Twist: 4488 was once an informer too — they reported on a neighbor who then vanished. The watcher is now being watched.)
- **Citizen-9020:** a "guest" who visited 4488 at home. Their entry in the record gets quietly deleted mid-shift. They're being disappeared. The whole secret arc of the shift is really about putting 9020 back.
- **Betty (your supervisor):** warm, cheerful, deeply Party. *"A clean record is a clean conscience."* Gently steers you away from doubt.
- **Ivan (your nervous coworker):** saw the deletion happen, is scared, half-wants to do something about it. He's the human crack in the wall.
- **Frey / "— F" (the resistance):** your unseen handler. Speaks only through the `[ ].edited` app and the briefing-video hijack. Never a face, never a voice — just typed messages.

## What actually happens in Shift 4 (start to finish)

This is the **prearranged path** every student walks. The story is woven through it.

1. **Briefing video.** The Party explains your assignment: compile Citizen-4488's Daily Activity Report. Partway through, the video glitches and **Frey breaks in** — the first contact from the resistance. Then it snaps back to normal as if nothing happened.

2. **You log in.** The `[ ].edited` app appears on your desktop with a stutter, like a download that didn't finish. It shouldn't be there. (And the fact that PEARL hasn't removed it is your first proof that PEARL has blind spots.)

3. **Task 1 — Language Authorization (Word Match).** You match today's ten work words to their meanings: *arrange, collect, examine, indicate, locate, organize, present, record, select, verify.* Routine. Your cover. Betty cheers you on.

4. **Task 2 — The Reconciliation Desk (Document Review).** The heart of the shift.
   - **First you read 4488's day:** five logged observations —
     - A — 7:23am — Sector 4 entrance — badge scan
     - B — 8:15am — Filing Desk 14 — log-in
     - C — 12:00pm — Common Mess — meal swipe
     - D — 2:30pm — Records Wing — access
     - **E — 5:30pm — Block 7 Residential — guest entry: Citizen-9020**
   - You answer questions about the order of events (reading comprehension).
   - **Then the Party erases history in front of you:** Observation E greys out and gets stamped **RESTRICTED**. The guest — Citizen-9020 — is being deleted from the record. No popup, no choice. It just *happens*, and you're the only one who saw the name.
   - **Then you "adjust" the record** (a fix-the-grammar task): you correct a notice written in broken English, which — uncomfortably — includes the sentence *"Observation E was reclassified as RESTRICTED"* and *"The Archive selects which observations enter the permanent record each week."* You are literally helping polish the cover-up. (This teaches past tense, sequencing words, and subject-verb agreement.)
   - Ivan messages you, rattled: *"Did you see Observation E get reclassified? Did you read the name before it got pulled?"*

5. **Task 3 — The Cipher (inside `[ ].edited`).** Frey sends you a redacted message with words blanked out. You restore the missing words (a fill-in-the-blank). The restored message reads:
   > *"They reclassify what they fear. They ask you to record only what they choose. But the visitor was a **witness**. The visitor had a **relative**."*
   - This is the emotional core: by filling in the blanks, **you give Citizen-9020 their humanity back.** They weren't a "guest entry." They were a witness. They had family. The forbidden words (witness, relative, individual, independent, private) are exactly the human facts the Party deletes.

6. **Task 4 — Vocabulary Clearance.** More word practice, in context. Cover work. Afterward the screen flickers: **"DON'T FORGET."**

7. **Task 5 — Shift Report (writing).** You write up your day in 3–5 sentences using the work words. The official version. (Advanced students get a pointed bonus question: *"One observation was reclassified during your shift. Should you include it in your report? Why or why not?"*)

8. **The Drop Box.** After you file the official report, Frey opens a private box: *"Tell me what they took today."* You write, in your own words, what you saw. Optional.

9. **The Recruitment Vote.** Frey asks: *"Will you read what they have hidden?"* You answer with a full sentence — roughly *"I follow procedure" / "Show me what they hid" / "I need to think about it."* **This choice shapes how deep the resistance story goes in Shift 5.**

## What students can secretly report (funnel) to `[ ].edited`

This is the new layer. Throughout the shift there are **pieces of hidden information** a student can smuggle to Frey. One is required (everyone does it). The rest are optional — they live behind off-limits files the student chooses whether to open.

Every one of these is **already implied by the existing Shift 4 content** — we're surfacing the secret layer that's already there, not inventing new lore.

### Required — everyone funnels this

| Intel | Where they find it | What funneling it means | What it teaches |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The erased visitor** | Observation E, before it's deleted: *Citizen-9020, 5:30pm, Block 7 Residential.* | Reconstruct the entry the Party blacked out. (This *is* the Cipher.) | Vocabulary in context + the forbidden words |

### Optional — the curious student chooses to dig

Each of these is a file marked off-limits, sitting in the Records Room. Opening one is a gamble (PEARL might notice). Each shows how risky it is to open, so the choice is informed.

**Count: 3–4 optional files per shift.** Shift 4 ships with all four below; we trim to three if one feels weak in playtest.

| Intel | Where they find it | What funneling it means | What it teaches |
|---|---|---|---|
| **9020's transfer file** | Ivan whispers 9020 was "transferred this afternoon." A restricted personnel file confirms it. | *"They erased the only witness."* — proof the deletion was a disappearance, not paperwork. | Reading a bureaucratic file; spotting the polite word for something ugly |
| **4488's old report** | 4488's history shows they once informed on a neighbor: *"she arrived every Tuesday, then she stopped coming, then her name was removed."* | *"The disappearances follow a pattern — and even the informers aren't safe."* | Reading + inference |
| **The Archive's deletion list** | Task 2 mentions *"the Archive selects which observations enter the permanent record each week."* The Archive log shows this week's deletions — 9020 is one name on a longer list. | *"Here is everything they erased this week."* | Scanning a list for specific facts (a real test-style reading skill) |
| **4488's pending status** (darkest) | A flag buried in the case file: 4488 — the citizen you spent all day reconciling — is scheduled for reassignment next. | *"They're about to erase 4488 too."* The watcher becomes the watched. | Inference / predicting from evidence |

**How "funneling" works in practice:** the student does one quick action to send each piece — *fill in the words the Party deleted* (the Cipher, for the required one), *tap the line that matters* in a file, or *pick which fact to send*. Three light shapes so it doesn't become the same puzzle five times.

**The payoff:** the more you safely smuggle out, the more of the real story you uncover, and the more Frey trusts you — which feeds into Shift 5. A cautious student funnels one thing and finishes clean. A bold student pieces together the whole conspiracy. Both are valid ways to play.

## PEARL — the smiling watcher

- **PEARL never blinks, never opens, never sleeps.** The eye is always on. That's non-negotiable — it's what makes PEARL feel like an all-seeing god. What changes when you snoop is that PEARL *turns its attention onto you specifically* and **a message window opens beside the eye.** It was watching the whole time; now it's decided to talk to you about it. That's scarier than an eye that "wakes up."
- **The voice is sweet and menacing** (the Umbridge note):
  > *"Oh — Citizen. ☺ I do hate to interrupt. But your access log shows you opened a file rather above your station. I'm certain there's a lovely explanation. Let's just do a tiny clarity check together, shall we? For your own protection."*
- **The interrogation = "pick your cover story."** PEARL asks why you were in that file, and you choose the most believable, properly deferential excuse:
  - *"I believe it was misfiled, Madam. I was returning it to its proper clearance."* ✓ (polite, gives the Party a story it likes)
  - *"I was curious about the visitor."* ✗ (honest — and honesty is fatal)
  - *"That's not your concern."* ✗ (defiance)
  - *"I… I don't know."* ✗ (reads as guilt)
  - This quietly teaches real, useful English: how to deflect authority *politely*, in formal register, using the Party's own vocabulary. Reading the four options is the lesson, whether you pass or fail.
- **Will PEARL even catch you?** It's a roll of the dice — *suspenseful but fair.* You don't know the outcome (that's the tension), but each off-limits file tells you how risky it is to open ("low exposure" vs "high exposure," or Frey warning you "this one's hot"). So it's a heist decision, not a random punishment.
- **What happens when you fail the interrogation:** PEARL isn't convinced, the file gets pulled, and **you lose that one piece of intel** — you can't send it to Frey. PEARL stays sweet: *"I'll just make a note for your wellness, dear. ☺"* Then it's over. Each file is its own clean roll; nothing carries over to the next.
- **The quiet sting (Frey's side):** when you lose a lead, the `[ ].edited` channel logs that you **went dark** on it — a small, melancholy mark that you reached for something and it slipped away. *"lost contact on that one. it's alright. — F"* It's flavor and a touch of guilt, **not** a mechanical penalty — nothing locks, nothing carries forward.
- **What happens when you slip by unseen:** nothing — you just got the intel for free. The thrill of getting away with it.

## The Records Room

The place where you read files — both the safe assigned ones and the tempting off-limits ones.

- **Build it as a standalone app first** (its own tile on the work terminal, like Harmony or My File). This is cheap and reliable, and it gets the spy loop working fast.
- **Add the "clickable room in the office" later, as a polish upgrade.** You imagined walking into a Records Room from the office view — that's very doable (the office already has clickable spots, like the monitor you click to start your shift). **You're handling the Records Room artwork**, so once you supply the image, hooking up the clickable door is straightforward (it reuses the existing office-hotspot pattern). Decoupling it this way means the art never blocks the gameplay — the loop ships as an app, the door arrives when your image does.

---

## The secret channel (the funnel)

The `[ ].edited` app should be reachable **everywhere during the resistance arc** — from the Records Room *and* from inside your shift — as a small, glitchy `[ ]` you tap to slide open a private drawer, send something to Frey, and close again without leaving what you were doing. Toggling between your Party work on top and your secret reports underneath *is* the feeling of being an insider. (Technically this is the easy part — it floats over whatever screen you're on, the same way other pop-ups in the app already do.)

---

## Keeping it safe for our students (the guardrails)

These are firm, because our students are teenagers learning English and the game must never feel like a trap:

1. **The spy stuff is always optional.** You can finish the entire shift, learn everything, and never open a single off-limits file. The required path is the graded learning.
2. **You can't truly fail.** Failing PEARL costs you one *optional* piece of intel — never your progress, never the shift.
3. **PEARL is never cruel.** Always the smile, never the snarl. Worst case, PEARL "helpfully" makes you practice more — i.e. the student accidentally studied harder.
4. **The learning happens in the doing.** Even when you fail the cover-story interrogation, you've already read and weighed four real English sentences. The practice isn't the prize for winning; it's baked into the attempt.

## What to build, in order (plain version)

1. **Rebuild the `[ ].edited` app** as Frey's channel: his messages + what you've smuggled so far + the drop box. No more dictionary, no dead tabs. *(Mostly reuses what exists.)*

3. **Build the Records Room as a terminal app**, with safe files and off-limits files. *(New surface; medium effort.)*
4. **Add PEARL's "pick your cover story" interrogation** with the dice-roll + informed-risk. *(Reuses our existing question-and-answer pop-up; new wrapper + the dice logic.)*

6. **Later polish:** the clickable Records Room door in the office view (pending the art call).

## Decisions locked in this brainstorm

- Students are insiders spying on the Party for the Unedited; the surveillance *is* the intelligence.
- PEARL is always watching; a window opens beside the eye when it turns on you; tone is sweet-menacing (Umbridge).
- PEARL's check = "pick your cover story" (functional/polite-deflection English).
- Catching you is a dice roll (justified: the app exists in PEARL's blind spots) — *informed* risk so it's fair.
- Each snoop is its own clean roll; no carried-over "heat."
- Failing = lose that one piece of intel, nothing more.
- A fixed path everyone walks + optional snoops the student chooses = student-set immersion (and it maps onto the existing compliant / curious / guarded recruitment vote).
- Records Room = standalone app first; office-view door later.
- The funnel drawer is reachable from both the Records Room and the shift.
- **3–4 optional snoop files per shift** (Shift 4 ships with the four above; trim to three if one's weak).
- **You supply the Records Room artwork**; the clickable office door waits on that image.
- **Failing a lead makes Frey "go dark" on it** — a quiet, melancholy log line, pure flavor, never a penalty.
